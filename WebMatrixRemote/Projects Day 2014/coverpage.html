Project Title Bioremediation of Strontium and Copper in water by Algae and Sea Urchins
Synopsis

With rapid industrialization, there has been a remarkable increase in the use of heavy metals over the past few decades, which resulted in an increased flux of metallic substances in the aquatic environment (Bishnoi, Anju, & Garima, 2004). These metals have a potential to accumulate within biological systems, and if their concentrations exceed allowable limits, it can pose health threats, to not only aquatic animals, but also land animals, including human beings. Algae are a large group of photoautotrophic organisms. They can survive in various kinds of conditions and are extremely easy to grow and can be readily used for bioremediation. In this project, dead and live Ulva was exposed to different concentrations of copper and strontium in water. The final concentration of heavy metal ions left in the water was measured after a trial period of five days. BThis project focuses on the bioremediation of non-radioactive Sr2+ and Cu2+ incorporating the use of algae and sea urchins, to explore a more economical method of remediation through the use of biomass.
